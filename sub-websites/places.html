Casco Viejo in Bilbao is the historic district of the city, known for its narrow, winding streets, colorful buildings, and bars and restaurants serving pintxos. It is the heart of old Bilbao, full of landmarks such as the Basilica of Saint James and traditional shops. The Plaza Nueva, a central square, often hosts local events and markets.

Guggenheim Museum is a world-renowned modern and contemporary art museum located in Bilbao, Spain. Designed by architect Frank Gehry, its striking, futuristic building is considered an architectural masterpiece.

A trip from Bilbao to Gaztelugatxe is a popular day excursion. Gaztelugatxe is a small rocky island on the Basque coast, famous for its dramatic scenery and winding stone staircase with over 200 steps leading to a hermitage at the top.

San Mamés Stadium is the home of Athletic Club Bilbao and one of the most iconic football stadiums in Spain. Known as "The Cathedral of Football," it opened in 2013 and hosts exciting matches as well as tours for visitors.

Mercado de la Ribera is a large and vibrant market located by the river in Bilbao's Old Town. It's one of the biggest covered markets in Europe, offering fresh local produce, seafood, meats, and traditional Basque specialties. The market also features bars and food stalls where you can enjoy pintxos and drinks in a lively atmosphere. It's a perfect spot to experience local culture and cuisine.

Plaza Nueva is a beautiful neoclassical square located in the heart of the Old Town (Casco Viejo). Built in the 19th century, it is surrounded by elegant arcades filled with lively bars, restaurants, and cafés. It’s a popular spot for locals and tourists to enjoy pintxos, relax, or attend small events and markets held in the square.

Maman is a famous sculpture by artist Louise Bourgeois, displayed in various locations around the world. In Bilbao, it is located outside the Guggenheim Museum. The sculpture depicts a giant spider, measuring over 9 meters in height, symbolizing themes of motherhood, protection, and fear. Bourgeois drew inspiration from her own relationship with her mother, as well as the nurturing and protective qualities of spiders. "Maman" has become one of the most iconic works of contemporary art.
